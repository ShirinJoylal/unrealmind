Young's Double-Slit Experiment

1. Introduction

The Young’s double-slit experiment demonstrates the wave nature of light by showing an interference pattern when light passes through two narrow slits.

2. Experimental Setup

  • Monochromatic light source (e.g., laser)
  • Two very narrow, closely spaced slits
  • A screen at distance D from the slits

3. Theory

Light from the two slits acts as coherent sources of waves. These waves overlap on the screen and produce an interference pattern of bright and dark fringes.

Path Difference: Δ = d sinθ
Condition for Bright Fringe: Δ = mλ
Condition for Dark Fringe: Δ = (m + ½)λ

For small angles (θ ≈ y/D):

ym = (m λ D) / d

The distance between two bright fringes (fringe width):

Δy = λD / d

4. Observations

- Alternating bright and dark fringes appear on the screen.
- Central bright fringe at θ = 0.
- Fringe spacing is uniform and depends on λ, D, and d.

5. Significance

This experiment provided strong evidence of the wave nature of light. In modern physics, repeating the experiment with electrons or photons shows wave-particle duality.
