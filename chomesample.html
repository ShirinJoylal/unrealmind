RELATIVITY

Quick Insight

Light always travels at the same speed, no matter the observer’s motion. This simple truth reshapes our understanding of time, space, and reality itself.

Special relativity describes how space and time are interwoven into a single continuum known as spacetime. It shows how measurements of them are relative to the observer’s motion. General relativity is the geometric theory of gravitation, describing how matter and energy influence spacetime’s curvature, how Earth and celestial bodies move in response, and how mass in the universe interacts through this curvature.

Einstein’s special theory of relativity (1905) defines the speed of light as a universal constant—the maximum speed for energy, matter, and information. Nothing can travel faster than light in a vacuum: about 299,792,458 m/s (≈186,282 miles/s).
